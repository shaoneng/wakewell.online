        How It Works: The Science of a Great Night's Sleep

        Our bedtime calculator isn't based on guesswork. It uses the science of 90-minute sleep cycles. A typical night's sleep consists of several cycles through different stages of sleep, including light sleep, deep sleep, and REM sleep. Waking up at the end of a cycle, rather than in the middle of one, is the key to avoiding sleep inertia (that groggy, morning-after feeling).

        Frequently Asked Questions

        How long is a human sleep cycle?

        A complete sleep cycle typically lasts about 90 minutes. Our calculator uses this as a baseline to recommend the best times to wake up.

        Is it better to wake up during light sleep?

        Yes. Waking up during a light sleep stage, which usually occurs at the end of a 90-minute cycle, helps you feel more alert and refreshed than being woken up from deep sleep.

        How much sleep do I really need?

        Most adults need 7-9 hours of sleep, which corresponds to 5-6 full sleep cycles. Our tool shows you options for 4, 5, and 6 cycles so you can choose what works for you.
